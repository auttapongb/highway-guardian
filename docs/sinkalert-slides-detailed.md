## SLIDE 5: LAYER 1 — Satellite InSAR

### WHAT IS InSAR?
Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar (InSAR) uses satellite radar to measure **millimeter-scale ground deformation** across wide areas.

### How It Works
1. **Sentinel-1 satellite** (European Space Agency) passes over Thailand every 6-12 days
2. Emits radar waves to Earth → measures the phase of reflected signal
3. Compare two passes at different times → **interferogram** showing exact ground movement
4. Generate **deformation rate map** — color-coded showing where ground is sinking

### Why It's Revolutionary for DOH

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Cost of data** | **FREE** (ESA Sentinel-1 — launched 2014, fully open) |
| **Coverage** | Satellite covers **entire Thai highway network** in one pass |
| **Precision** | **±1.5 mm/year** vs ground benchmarks (verified by multiple studies) |
| **Frequency** | Updated every **6-12 days** per satellite |
| **History** | More satellites joining: future revisit = **3-5 days** (Sentinel-1C) |
| **Processing** | Open-source: **PySAR / MintPy** (Python, GitHub, MIT license) |

### Thailand-Specific Research (Published)
- **PS-InSAR Measurement of Land Subsidence in Bangkok Metropolitan Area** — Academia.edu
- **Land subsidence in Bangkok vicinity: Causes and long-term trends** — ScienceDirect (2024)
- **Land Surface Displacement in Bangkok, Thailand Inferred from InSAR** — Multiple institutions
- Research conducted at: **AIT (Asian Institute of Technology)** and various Thai universities
- Verified accuracy: **±1.5 mm/yr vs ground benchmark leveling** at 6/10 test benchmarks

### Current Known Subsidence Hotspots (InSAR can detect)
- Bangkok avg: 10mm/yr sinking
- Ramkhamhaeng Rd: 20mm/yr — highest rate
- If this rate continues: **Bangkok could be under water by 2030** (NRC Thailand)

### The Software (ALL Open Source)
- **YOLOv8 Nano** (Ultralytics) → trained on road damage datasets
- Converted to **TensorFlow Lite** (quantized INT8) → runs at 4-6 FPS on Pi
- **RDD2022 dataset** — 47,420 road images from 6 countries (Japan, India, Czech, Norway, USA, China)
- **PaveSAM** (arXiv) — Segment Anything Model adapted for pavement distress
- **YOLO-ROC** (arXiv:2507.23225) — ultra-lightweight real-time road damage detection

### Detection Targets
| Damage Type | What It Indicates | Risk Level |
|-------------|------------------|------------|
| Surface cracks | Soil movement below | Medium-High |
| Depressions / sagging | Subsurface void forming | HIGH |
| Water pooling | Drainage failure, erosion risk | Medium |
| Potholes | Advanced surface failure | Low-Medium |
| Edge erosion | Shoulder instability | Medium |

### Why DOH Already Has This
- DOH survey vehicles **already drive every highway section** regularly
- No new vehicle procurement needed — **just add $80 kit**
- Survey vehicles already have staff, schedule, and 4G access
- **Pilot: 10 vehicles × $80 = $800 total for hardware**

### Proven Open-Source Base
- `SubhayanBiswas/Anomaly-Detection-Dashcam-Raspberrypi` — RPi + TFLite + Flask dashboard
- `PeterHdd/pothole-detection-yolo` — YOLOv8 + FastAPI + React Native
- Academic studies show **80-95%+ accuracy** for pothole/crack detection with YOLO

## SLIDE 7: LAYER 3 + ML FUSION — THE INTELLIGENCE

### LAYER 3: Environmental Data (The Triggers)

| Data Source | Format | Update Frequency | Why It Matters |
|------------|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Rainfall** — Thai Met Dept | API, hourly | **Realtime** | Water erodes soil → triggers collapse |
| **Soil type** — DMR Thailand | Shapefile | Static | Clay soil = higher risk |
| **Soil moisture** — Satellite + sensors | Grid, daily | Daily | Saturation weakens ground |
| **Traffic load** — DOH data | CSV, monthly | Monthly | Heavy trucks accelerate weakening |
| **Temperature** — Met Dept | API, hourly | Realtime | Heat-dry cycles crack pavement |
| **Historical collapses** — DOH records | GIS points | Static | Training data for ML model |

**Real-world proof:** The Samsen Road collapse (Sep 2025) was **directly triggered by heavy rainfall** — soil erosion into the MRT tunnel, which then created the 50m deep sinkhole.

### ML FUSION — XGBoost Model

**Input Features:**
```
X = [
    insar_deformation_rate_mm_year,   # Layer 1 — satellite
    dashcam_damage_score,              # Layer 2 — CV
    rainfall_24h_mm,                   # Layer 3 — weather
    rainfall_7d_total,                 # Layer 3 — weather
    soil_moisture_pct,                 # Layer 3 — soil
    soil_type_encoded,                 # Layer 3 — geology
    traffic_load_tons_day,             # Layer 3 — load
    temperature_celsius,               # Layer 3 — climate
    days_since_last_inspection,        # DOH operational
]

y = collapse_risk_score_0_100
```

**Algorithm:** XGBoost (eXtreme Gradient Boosting)
- Industry standard for structured/tabular data
- Handles missing values (not every road has all data)
- Feature importance output → tells you WHY a segment is high risk
- Training time: < 5 minutes on historical data
